# Tutorial Design Patterns

A tutorial is a staged handoff from observation to independent play. The strongest pattern teaches a
small action in the real context, lets the player perform it immediately, gives readable feedback, and
removes assistance as competence grows.

## Why it works

Short, contextual prompts reduce working-memory load and connect instruction to a visible goal. Optional
repetition, practice spaces, and gradual complexity let players recover without blocking experienced
players.

## The catch

Forced walls of text, irreversible early choices, and tutorials that never relinquish control increase
drop-off. A skippable tutorial can also hide necessary information if the game lacks discoverable help.

## In practice here

Teach one verb at a time, measure the first-session funnel, let returning players skip or replay lessons,
and test comprehension through action rather than a quiz.
