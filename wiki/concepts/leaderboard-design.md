# Leaderboard Design

A leaderboard turns a measurable performance into a social comparison. Good design defines the scoring
window, ranking population, tie rules, visibility, and rewards so competition feels legible and fair.

## Why it works

Short seasons create fresh starting points, while friend or local brackets give players achievable
comparisons. A stable score definition and auditable submission path make improvement meaningful.

## The catch

Global rankings privilege extreme spend or time, stale boards discourage newcomers, and rewards can
invite collusion, smurfing, or cheating. Resetting without preserving history can also erase achievement.

## In practice here

Use seasonal or cohort-based boards, show percentile and local rank, validate submissions server-side,
preserve historical records, and reward participation or improvement as well as the top position.
