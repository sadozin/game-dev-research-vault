# Dual Currency Design

A dual-currency economy separates a commonly earned soft currency from a scarcer hard currency used
for premium access, acceleration, or special purchases. The separation can protect everyday progression
from monetization pressure, but only if the currencies have distinct jobs and understandable exchange.

## Why it works

Soft currency supports play-earned pacing and sinks; hard currency supports premium choice without
putting a cash price on every basic action. Clear conversion boundaries let designers tune retention,
spending, and inflation separately.

## The catch

Too many currencies increase cognitive load, obscure prices, and make value comparisons difficult.
Hard-currency gates on core progression can read as pay-to-win, while generous conversion leaks can
collapse the intended scarcity.

## In practice here

Give each currency a distinct purpose, show real-money equivalents, keep core play viable without
purchase, and audit balances by cohort, source, sink, and conversion path.
