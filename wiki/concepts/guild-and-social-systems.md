# Guild and Social Systems

Guilds, parties, friends, shared goals, and social recognition turn a game from an individual activity
into a network of commitments. Their value comes from repeated useful interaction, not merely adding a
chat channel or a guild badge.

## Why it works

Shared objectives create reasons to return and make a player's absence visible to people they know.
Roles, contribution feedback, and low-friction invitations help groups coordinate without requiring
every member to be equally skilled or available.

## The catch

Social obligation can become pressure, harassment, exclusion, or a barrier to solo players. Guild rewards
can amplify runaway advantages when established groups recruit the strongest members.

## In practice here

Support small-group and solo paths, give leaders moderation tools, make contribution legible without
ranking people only by spend or hours, and measure social features against healthy long-term retention.
