# MMO combat roles

Combat roles are the useful contributions a player makes to a group, such as absorbing pressure,
restoring allies, defeating targets, controlling enemies, enabling allies, scouting, or moving
the party through the world. A class system succeeds when each class has a legible contribution
to the encounters the game actually asks groups to solve.

## Why it works

The tank/healer/DPS trinity makes damage management easy to understand and gives group encounters
clear dependencies. It is not the only possible structure: crowd control, buffs, debuffs,
mobility, information, melee/ranged reach, and area versus single-target output can all be
meaningful dimensions. Start encounter design by naming the outputs it requires, then create
classes and [[mmo-skill-loadouts]] that can produce those outputs in distinct ways.

Treat the party as an economy. A player spending a global cooldown or resource on healing is not
dealing damage, and a player providing control lets others spend fewer resources on survival.
The game becomes cooperative when these trades are real and when encounters demand different
combinations of them rather than simply more damage.

## The catch

Do not copy the trinity because it is familiar. A game with solo-first content, no threat system,
or highly mobile action combat may need a different coordination model. But removing dedicated
healers or tanks does not remove the design problem: decide who prevents failure, who recovers
from mistakes, and why other players are valuable.

## In practice here

[[micro-mmo]] has no documented combat or party model yet. Before inventing classes, define the
largest intended party, primary activity, encounter failure states, and whether solo players must
complete the same content. Those decisions select the appropriate role structure.
