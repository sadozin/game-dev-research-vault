# Hybrid class balance

A hybrid class can contribute to more than one [[mmo-combat-roles|combat role]]. Its balance
problem is structural: if it matches a specialist in one role and adds another, the specialist is
obsolete; if it is always weaker than specialists, players have no reason to choose it.

## Why it works

Give a hybrid a valuable combination of partial outputs when the party size or encounter changes
make that combination useful. Small groups cannot adjust role supply in fine increments, so a
character that is meaningfully competent in two areas can fit a gap that whole specialists cannot.
Different kinds of the same output—such as protection against physical versus magical pressure—
also create niches that are not reducible to one damage or healing number.

Use varying encounter phases and objectives to make flexibility valuable during play, not only in
the character creator. A hybrid's tradeoff must be visible: choosing to heal costs damage,
defensive gear costs offense, or a limited [[mmo-skill-loadouts|loadout]] cannot cover every
situation simultaneously.

## The catch

Making every class self-sufficient for solo progression pushes every class toward hybridization.
Support solo play with a baseline survival and damage floor, but reserve peak group efficiency and
signature utility for specialization. Otherwise numerical tuning will repeatedly discover a
dominant "tank-mage" build after players combine the strongest offense and defense.

## In practice here

For [[micro-mmo]], begin with a few small-group scenarios and a contribution matrix: rows are
classes or archetypes; columns are sustained damage, burst, mitigation, recovery, control,
mobility, and utility. Every proposed hybrid needs a purposeful row before implementation.
