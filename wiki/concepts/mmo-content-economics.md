# MMO content economics

MMO content economics compares player-hours consumed with team-hours required to author, integrate,
test, localize, operate, and refresh them. A quest treadmill is structurally hostile to a tiny team
because consumption scales with players while authored output scales with developers.

Replayable systems improve the ratio: social goals, contested resources, procedural layouts, rotating
modifiers, mastery, build choices, dynamic events, and player rivalry can create different experiences
from the same validated content set. They still require curation and anti-exploit testing; procedural
generation moves work into rules and validation rather than eliminating it.

Measure content cost as a production metric. Record hours from brief to playable, QA defects, player
completion time, replay rate, and maintenance burden. If the sustainable monthly output cannot support
the promised cadence, change the game design before expanding the team or automating more content.
