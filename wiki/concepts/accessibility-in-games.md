# Accessibility in games

Game accessibility removes avoidable barriers so players can perceive, operate, understand, and
customize the experience.

## Why it works

Redundant signals, remappable input, readable presentation, captions, and adjustable timing let
players use their strengths instead of forcing one sensory or motor path.

## The catch

An option that exists but is hidden, incomplete, or unavailable during a critical sequence is not a
reliable accommodation. Accessibility can also be undermined by communicating only through color.

## In practice

Test with disabled players, expose settings early, preserve them across saves, and audit every
critical mechanic for alternate input and feedback paths.
