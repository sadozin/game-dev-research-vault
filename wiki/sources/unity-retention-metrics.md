# Source: Unity — Analytics dashboards

- **Author:** Unity Technologies
- **Published:** Current documentation (last updated 2026-07-09)
- **URL:** https://docs.unity.com/en-us/analytics/dashboards/dashboards
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-11

## Summary

Unity's dashboards define player retention as the average percentage of players who play on each day
after installation, with examples such as D7 retention. The dashboard can filter by country, version,
platform, and audience, and Unity notes that a gradual curve and steep drop indicate different
retention shapes. The practical lesson is to measure cohorts and segments rather than treat a single
active-user total as evidence that a retention loop works.
