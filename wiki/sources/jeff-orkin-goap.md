# Source: Jeff Orkin Goal-Oriented Action Planning

- **Author:** Jeff Orkin
- **Published:** 2003-2006
- **URL:** http://alumni.media.mit.edu/~jorkin/goap.html
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-11

## Summary

GOAP represents actions by their preconditions and effects, then searches for a sequence that reaches
a goal from the current world state. It shifts authored behavior from “if this, do that” branches to
action vocabulary and world facts. Planning cost, incomplete knowledge, and brittle effects still
need bounds and designer constraints.
