# Source: Official online-service privacy and platform duties

- **Authors:** European Commission; United States Federal Trade Commission
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-14
- **Raw:** raw/online-service-privacy-and-platform-duties.md (provenance only)

## Summary

Official EU guidance describes rights over personal data including access, correction, erasure,
restriction, objection, and portability. The Digital Services Act adds platform duties whose exact
application depends on the service, including accessible reporting, explanations for moderation
decisions, appeal paths, and protections for minors. The US COPPA rule applies to child-directed online
services and general-audience services with actual knowledge that they collect personal information
from children under 13.

These are architecture inputs, not a compliance checklist. Data export and deletion become harder after
identity data spreads across stores; chat and user-generated content create moderation operations; and
audience decisions can change privacy obligations. A launch plan needs qualified, jurisdiction-specific
legal advice.
