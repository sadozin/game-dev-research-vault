# Source: MDA — A Formal Approach to Game Design and Game Research

- **Authors:** Robin Hunicke, Marc LeBlanc, Robert Zubek
- **Published:** 2004
- **URL:** https://www.cs.northwestern.edu/~hunicke/MDA.pdf
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-14
- **Raw:** raw/mda-game-design-framework.md (provenance only)

## Summary

MDA separates a game into mechanics, the run-time dynamics produced when those mechanics interact,
and the player experience or aesthetics those dynamics evoke. Designers usually approach from rules
toward experience, while players encounter the chain in reverse. Small algorithm or data changes can
therefore cascade into very different play.

For tower defense, range, costs, targeting, routes, and wave schedules are mechanics; kill zones,
hoarding, specialization, recovery, and dominant openings are dynamics; anticipation, mastery,
clarity, and dramatic relief are desired experiences. The framework is most useful as a causal check:
every intended feeling needs supporting dynamics, and every dynamic needs an observable mechanic.
