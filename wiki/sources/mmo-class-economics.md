# Source: Hopson — MMO class economics

- **Author:** John Hopson
- **Published:** 2008-04-18
- **URL:** https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/mmo-class-design-up-with-hybrids-an-economic-argument
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-11
- **Raw:** `raw/mmo-class-economics.md` (provenance only)

## Summary

Treat a party as an economy. Its players supply and consume damage, durability, healing, crowd
control, buffs, and utility; casting a skill has explicit resource costs and implicit opportunity
cost. A pure specialist tends to dominate one commodity, while a hybrid must have a contribution
that remains valuable without matching every specialist at its specialty.

The source explains why hybrids are structurally difficult. A hybrid that tanks as well as a
tank while also healing replaces the tank; one that is always worse at every individual role is
never selected. It recommends designing varied encounter demands, non-interchangeable forms of
the same output, and party constraints deliberately so hybrid flexibility has a real niche.
