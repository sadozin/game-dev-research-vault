# Source: Taugrim — Action combat and non-threat tanking

- **Author:** Ed Park (Taugrim)
- **Published:** 2014-01-26 (action combat); 2013-10-27 (threat critique)
- **URL:** https://taugrim.com/2014/01/26/wildstar-advances-the-action-combat-paradigm-for-mmorpgs/
- **Also:** https://taugrim.com/2013/10/27/threat-based-pve-is-lame-and-how-id-fix-it/
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-12

## Summary

UI-based MMO combat centres on range checks, auto-hit abilities, long rotations, and cooldown UI;
player attention sits on bars and meters. Action combat (as popularised in TERA/GW2 and pushed
further in WildStar’s movement/aim/CC “sandwich”) lets abilities fire without a valid lock, demands
player aim, and treats positioning and timing as the skill axis. Free-form targeting and enemy
telegraphs make hitting and avoiding skill-based rather than pure stat checks—at the risk of
telegraph spam that replaces bar-watching with floor-watching.

A companion argument attacks *threat-table* tanking specifically: sticky aggro feels arbitrary,
trains tank-and-spank, and keeps eyes on meters. An alternative frames tanks as mobile protectors
(body blocks, control tools, coordinated movement with the party) while bosses can switch targets
fluidly. Tanking then becomes a team job—DPS and healers must move with the scheme—and can be built
from abilities rather than only threat, mitigation, and HP numbers, so more compositions can cover
protection without a dedicated taunt specialist.
