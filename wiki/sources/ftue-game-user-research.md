- **Published:** varies by article

## Summary

Good onboarding teaches the smallest useful action in context, lets the player perform it, and
provides feedback before adding another rule. It should reveal the game’s core loop rather than
front-load every system. The tradeoff is coverage: a tutorial that explains everything can delay
the motivating play, while a thin one can leave players without a goal model.
