## Summary

The thesis treats paths, tower positions, and enemy waves as separable components and investigates
procedural generation and playtesting. Path length, turning, linearity, branches, tower coverage,
wave timing, resources, and enemy abilities all affect estimated difficulty.

Longer or more turning paths often make a level easier by increasing exposure and cross-coverage,
while short straight paths can increase pressure. These are tendencies within the studied designs,
not universal laws. Evaluation strategy itself can bias the difficulty estimate, and numerical
balance still requires player feedback.
