# Source: McDonald — Standard MMO character roles

- **Author:** Joshua McDonald
- **Published:** 2010-07-30
- **URL:** https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/standard-mmo-character-roles-the-good-and-the-bad
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-12

## Summary

Strict tank/healer/DPS roles make pickup groups legible: strangers can fill slots without
negotiation, and players can advertise value as a single number (DPS, gear score). The cost is
shallow mid-fight decision-making—even complex bosses often reduce to a known checklist—and weak
support for stylized off-meta builds once “best stats for role” dominate.

Simply adding more mandatory roles (CC, buffer) is a weak fix: queues get harder while combat
remains scripted. Stronger moves change aggro rules and class breadth so it is not always optimal
to park every hit on one tank, and so classes can do more than one useful thing. Without a taunt,
high-damage characters can peel by forcing target switches; glass cannons must watch the fight
rather than fire endlessly. That style rewards fixed groups who learn each other and hurts random
parties who cannot agree on the plan.
