## Summary

Blender defines materials through networks of nodes that output values, vectors, colors, and shaders.
The Shader Editor edits node trees used by render engines such as Eevee and Cycles, with material
outputs providing surface, volume, and displacement targets. Node setups are therefore engine- and
output-sensitive rather than just arbitrary graph data.
