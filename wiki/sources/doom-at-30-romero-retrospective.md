## Summary

Romero describes Doom spaces through large rooms, stairs, light and dark contrast, windows, ledges,
secret corridors, and glimpses of desirable destinations. He also describes a rhythm that alternates
combat with exploration and space to stop, absorb the environment, and find the route.

This creator testimony rejects the caricature that classic Doom is uninterrupted combat. Loops,
landmarks, previews, secrets, and recovery beats are part of its level-design language.
