# Source: A NEAT Approach to Wave Generation in Tower Defense Games

- **Authors:** Daniel Hind, Carlo Harvey
- **Published:** 2022, IMET
- **URL:** https://www.open-access.bcu.ac.uk/13568/
- **DOI:** 10.1109/IMET54801.2022.9929595
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-14
- **Raw:** raw/neat-tower-defense-wave-generation.md (provenance only)

## Summary

The study trained a NEAT network to observe a tower-defense state and generate waves intended to
challenge the current defense. A blind comparison used the Games Experience Questionnaire against
human-authored waves. Reported player experience showed no discernible difference, supporting the
feasibility of learned wave candidates, but evidence that the method increased engagement was
inconclusive.

Generated or adaptive waves are therefore a production aid, not proof of good pacing. A designer
still owns teaching order, telegraphing, strategy diversity, recovery beats, and the promise made to
the player about whether the game reacts to their build.
