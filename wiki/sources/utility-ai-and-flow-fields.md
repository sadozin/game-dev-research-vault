# Source: utility AI and flow-field pathfinding references

- **Author:** Dave Mark; Amit Patel
- **Published:** 2009 / continuously maintained
- **URL:** https://www.redblobgames.com/pathfinding/
- **Retrieved:** 2026-07-11

## Summary

Utility AI turns considerations into comparable scores and chooses the best current action, making
continuous priorities easier to express than a large hand-authored tree. Flow fields reverse the
usual many-agent pathfinding cost: compute a shared cost/direction field for a destination, then
let many agents follow it. Both techniques still need authoring constraints and stability rules.
